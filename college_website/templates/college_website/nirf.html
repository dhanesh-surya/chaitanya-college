About NIRF Rankings

{% if nirf_info and nirf_info.overview %}
{{ nirf_info.overview|safe }}
{% else %}

The National Institutional Ranking Framework (NIRF) was approved by the MHRD and launched by Honourable Minister of Human Resource Development on 29th September 2015. This framework outlines a methodology to rank institutions across the country based on specific parameters.

The ranking framework is based on five broad generic parameters:

  • Teaching, Learning and Resources (TLR): Faculty-student ratio, combined metric for faculty with PhD and Experience
  • Research and Professional Practice (RP): Publications, citations, patents, funded projects
  • Graduation Outcomes (GO): University examinations, median salary, number of students placed
  • Outreach and Inclusivity (OI): Percentage of students from other states/countries, women, economically and socially challenged students
  • Perception (PR): Peer perception, employer perception

NIRF Ranking Methodology

The NIRF ranking methodology is based on five key parameters with specific weightages:

  • Teaching, Learning and Resources (30%): Student strength, faculty-student ratio, combined metric for faculty with PhD
  • Research and Professional Practice (30%): Publications, citations, patents, consultancy projects
  • Graduation Outcomes (20%): University examinations, median salary, number of PhD students graduated
  • Outreach and Inclusivity (10%): Regional diversity, women diversity, economically and socially challenged students
  • Perception (10%): Peer perception of academic peers and employers
